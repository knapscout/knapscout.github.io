Waypoint01

What KnapScout Does

Walk the ground. Drop pins. Log finds. Swap data without losing track of whose is whose.

KnapScout is a working tool, not a marketing exercise. It captures where you walked, what you saw, and what you picked up — all on your phone, all in one place. No app store, no account, no fees. Open it in a browser, save it to your home screen, and head to the field.

  • GPS path tracking so you can see exactly where you covered
  • Three pin types for the three things you actually find: nothing, evidence, and finds
  • Owner-tagged exports so a group hunt doesn't turn into a data mess
  • Photos, notes, and coordinates saved with every find
Waypoint02

Open the App

One URL. No install. No account.

Open your phone's browser and type in the address. When the app loads, hit the share icon and choose Add to Home Screen. After that, it lives next to your other apps — one tap, full screen, ready to go.

Once the app has loaded once, the core functions work offline. Useful when you're a mile back into a clear cut with one bar of signal.

Waypoint03

Set Your Username

Your name on every pin you drop. The trick that makes group hunts work.

The first time you open the app, it asks for your name or initials. Whatever you enter gets stamped onto every pin you create from then on.

This is the feature that separates KnapScout from a generic mapping app. When you import a hunting partner's export into your own data, the owner tag stays attached. Your pins stay yours. Theirs stay theirs. No more arguing over who found the broken Halifax.

Waypoint04

Pick Your Map Layer

Three views of the same ground. Use them all.

Map

Roads, parcels, orientation. Find your way in and out.

Satellite

Spot fresh clear cuts, plowed fields, eroded banks.

Topo

Read terraces, drainages, confluences. The one that matters most.

  • Map view — roads, parcels, orientation. Use it to find your way in and out.
  • Satellite — for spotting fresh clear cuts, plowed fields, eroded banks, and other recent disturbance you can hunt.
  • Topo — the one that matters most. Read elevated terraces, drainages, confluences, and south-facing slopes. The map tells you where to look before you put a boot down.

Switch layers on the fly. You'll often plan a walk on topo, navigate in on map, and confirm your position against satellite once you're working the ground.

Waypoint05

Drop Pins as You Walk

Three pin types covering everything you encounter.

The pin system is the backbone of the app. Use it for every meaningful spot — including the spots where you found nothing. Negative data is real data.

No Evidence

Ground walked. Nothing seen. Proves you covered it. Lets you skip it next visit, or come back when conditions change.

Evidence

Flakes, debitage, fire-cracked rock. Anything that says people worked here. Marks the productive zones for follow-up.

Find

The keeper. Photo, notes, GPS — captured the moment you pick it up. Reads the site at a glance once you have a few logged.

Waypoint06

Track Your Walk

Hit start. Hunt. Read your line later.

Tap start when you begin walking the ground. The phone records your path automatically — every step, every backtrack, every detour around a brushpile. When you're done, you can see exactly where you covered and, more importantly, where you didn't.

Watch the GPS accuracy reading in the app — it shows in meters. Under open sky on a flat field, expect 3-5 meters. Heavy canopy or steep terrain can push it to 15-20. A pin is only as good as the meter reading at the moment you dropped it, so check before you commit a find.

Waypoint07

Log a Find

The moment you pick it up — not in the truck on the drive home.

Drop a Find pin where you stand. The app captures GPS automatically. Snap a photo right there in your palm — same lighting every time, scale provided by your hand. Add notes while it's fresh: material, condition, anything diagnostic.

  • Material first: quartzite, quartz, chert, rhyolite, jasper
  • Then condition: complete, distal, medial, proximal, base
  • Then anything you can read: type if you know it, fracture, patina, edge wear

You'll thank yourself later. Field memory fades fast, and a pile of unlogged finds in a baggie is just clutter.

Waypoint08

Share & Merge

Send your day out. Bring their day in. Keep the lines clean.

Tap export and the iOS share sheet does the rest. AirDrop to a hunting partner, email it to yourself, save it to Files for backup, drop it in a group chat. Whatever your workflow already is, the export plays with it.

When a partner sends you their export, hit import. The app brings their pins into your map without overwriting anything. Owner tags ride along on every pin, so you can always see who logged what — even months later, even after a dozen merges.

Waypoint09

Field Tips

Lessons from real walks. Save yourself the mistakes.

Watch the meters

Check the GPS accuracy before logging important finds. Under 5m is gold. Over 15m, wait it out or note the drift in the find notes.

Bring a battery pack

GPS tracking eats power. A 10,000 mAh brick will run you all day with margin.

Pin early, pin often

Drop more pins than you think you need. You can always delete in the truck. You can't add what you didn't notice.

Photo in the palm

Same lighting, same scale, every time. Your hand is a built-in measuring stick once you get used to it.

Export before you leave

Hit export at the truck — not at home. Cellular hiccups have eaten data before. Get it off the phone while you can still see the site.
